📖 Value bets, edge & Kelly — quick guide

Value bet — The bookmaker’s price implies a lower chance of winning than our model does. If we think an outcome is more likely than the odds suggest, there may be a positive edge.

Edge % — Our model probability minus the bookmaker’s implied probability (from decimal odds). Example: we say 55%, the book implies 45% → +10% edge. We only flag value when edge clears a safety margin (not every small difference).

ROI % — Expected long-run return on that bet if our probabilities are right: roughly edge relative to implied chance. Higher ROI means a stronger statistical advantage on paper — not a guarantee.

Kelly % — A staking guide, not a prediction. It suggests what fraction of your bankroll might be reasonable for this edge, using a fractional Kelly (conservative: we use 25% of full Kelly, capped at 10% of bankroll). Labels:

  • Strong — Clear edge; larger (still capped) suggested stake.
  • Moderate — Usable edge; moderate suggested stake.
  • Cautious — Thin edge; small suggested stake only.
  • Skip — No positive edge; do not bet for value on this line.
